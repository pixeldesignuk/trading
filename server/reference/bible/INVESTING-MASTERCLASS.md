## Mindset first (Concepts 0–1)

Before any allocation, Zero frames *why* you invest. Captured for completeness; the hub doesn't model these, but they're the foundation he builds on.

- **Concept 0 — Default vs Intentional path.** Society runs a "mechanical trap" (school → job → debt → repeat → retire tired) reinforced by a "psychological trap" (lifestyle spend, comparison loops, cultural timelines — "by 30 you should…"). The result: success defined externally, identity compressed to "I am my results", motivation extrinsic. Naming the script is step one.
- **Concept 1 — Escaping the trap.** *"You don't escape by rejecting the system — you escape by becoming less dependent on it."* Four levers: **awareness** (most paths are decided, not chosen), **deconditioning** (detach self-worth from grades/salary/social validation), **reclaiming control** (design paths, make small independent decisions), and the exit mechanism — **financial decoupling** (multiple income streams, ownership) + **skill leverage** (high-value non-linear skills: communication, sales, building, content — output not tied to time). Investing is one of these decoupling streams.

## The framework

### Foundation first (Concept 2 — "Operational framework")
- Build the base **before** investing: **6–12 months of expenses** in cash, at a **20–50% savings rate** early on. Capital = optionality. Don't invest money you can't afford to lose.
- **Invest consistently** (weekly/monthly DCA) — automate it, make it a habit. Stop obsessing over price.
- **Control costs & taxes** — low TER, efficient (accumulating, ISA-wrapped) structure; avoid unnecessary trading. *Ask yourself: "Do I want to trade, or do I want to make money?"*
- **Reinvest the majority** — dividends + gains back into the system. *Compounding only works if you don't quit; small amounts scale massively over time.* Wealth = (income + savings + investments) × **time** × **discipline**. Time is the main multiplier; everything else is secondary.
- **Stay consistent through cycles — drawdown is a buy signal, scaled:** **−20%** (normal correction) → stay consistent; **−30%** → lean in (increase DCA if the thesis holds); **−50%** → maximum buying, *provided fundamentals are intact*. Crashes are part of the game; most wealth is built in the boring years. Discipline > intelligence.
- **Enjoy the journey + share** — be happy about both setbacks and wins; help others doing the same; take breaks. Think in **decades**, not quarters.

### 🪐 Solar System — portfolio construction (Concept 3)
- **Core 60–80%** (target ~**70%**) — broad, global, low-cost ETFs = the compounding engine. Zero's template split: **40% global all-world** + **15% US (S&P 500)** + **15% quality-income/dividend**. Rationale: own the whole market (diversification) + overweight the strongest economy (growth) + add quality & income (stability + cash flow).
- **Satellites 20–40%** — boosters that **fund the core** (rotate profits inward). Per-sleeve target bands from the slide:
  - **Tech / Innovation 8–12%** — big-tech / Nasdaq, semis.
  - **Emerging Markets 5–8%** — broad EM (large/mid/small).
  - **Commodities 5–8%** — gold (physical), broad commodities, individual metals/energy.
  - **Niche themes 3–6%** — clean energy, biotech, gaming/metaverse, etc.
  - **Crypto 2–5%** — BTC/ETH + alts (high-risk / asymmetric upside).
- **Priority to core**; add satellites only once you understand rotation. **ETF due-diligence checklist:** spot- vs futures-based (contango/rollover risk), bid-ask spread **< 0.25%**, AUM **≥ ~£500m**.
- **Dividend strategy — three levels** as the book matures: **(A) Reinvest** into the most underweight ETF → accelerate compounding (growth phase); **(B) Rebalance** → buy the weakest part of the portfolio (equilibrium); **(C) Extraction** → withdraw dividends for cash flow / enjoy life (final level).

> ⚠️ **Sharia note:** Zero's example tickers (VWCE, S&P 500, QQQ, SMH, EIMI, SGLD, INRG, IBB, ESPO…) are the **structural template, not Sharia-screened.** The **percentage bands carry over; the tickers don't** — swap in the halal GBP-quoted equivalents (ISWD/HIWS core, HIES EM, SGLN gold…). See the practical-learnings section below.

### 🔺 Pyramid of Risk — **a framework *inside the satellite*** (NOT portfolio-wide)
> ⚠️ **Critical structural point (transcript L244–248):** *"it's a framework **inside the satellite**… this pyramid… high risk, five, 10%… **5, 10% of the satellite**."* The pyramid is **how you structure the satellite sleeve (the 20–40%)** by risk — it is **not** a second allocation of the whole portfolio. **The tier %s below are % *of the satellite budget*, not of the portfolio.** Core (60–80%) sits outside the pyramid entirely; it's the stable compounding base. Cash sits at the pyramid's defensive base.

Within the satellite sleeve, split by risk tier:
- **High 5–10% of satellite** — early-stage startups, microcaps, pre-profit DTC brands, turnaround/restructuring plays. **← individual stock picks (active trades) live here**, the tip.
- **Moderate 25–35% of satellite** — mid-cap discretionary growth, premium/trend-driven brands. Very sensitive to consumer sentiment → can 3–5× quickly **or collapse**. (e.g. XLY, Nike, Adidas, Puma — cyclical brands.)
- **Lower 30–40% of satellite** — consumer staples: stable cash flow, hold up in downturns. (e.g. Coca-Cola, Walmart, Procter & Gamble.)
- **Defensive base 10–20% of satellite** — utilities, healthcare, **cash** (XLP/XLU/XLV + cash). Low volatility, preserves capital, provides dry powder to rotate up when the cycle turns.

So a satellite holding has **two coordinates**: its *theme* (tech/EM/commodities/niche/crypto) **and** its *pyramid tier* (how risky). Rotation (next) moves capital **up and down these tiers** as the GDP cycle turns — the core never moves.

### 🔄 Macro Rotation
- The **GDP cycle** moves money up/down the pyramid:
  - **Expansion** (GDP accelerating, confidence rising, liquidity supportive) → money flows **bottom→top**: staples → discretionary → high-risk caps.
  - **Late cycle / slowdown** (growth positive but slowing, margins peaking, rates restrictive) → rotate **top→middle→bottom**; lock gains into staples + cash.
  - **Recession** (contracting GDP, rising unemployment, tightening credit) → concentrate capital at the **defensive base** of the pyramid.
  - **Recovery** → climb the pyramid again. Always check GDP.
- Read it with **"the Big 6"** — **6 sector *groups*** spanning the 11 S&P SPDR sector ETFs:
  1. **Defensive** — XLP (staples), XLU (utilities), XLV (health care)
  2. **Cyclical** — XLY (discretionary), XLI (industrials), XLB (materials)
  3. **Growth** — XLK (technology), XLC (communication services)
  4. **Financials** — XLF (banks/insurance, driven by rates & credit)
  5. **Energy** — XLE (oil & gas, driven by commodity prices & geopolitics)
  6. **Real Estate** — XLRE (REITs, sensitive to interest rates)
- Compare **ratios**: **XLY/XLP** (consumer strength vs defense), **XLK/XLU** (growth vs safety), **XLF/XLV** (cyclical vs defensive). All rising → risk-on expansion; mixed → late cycle, be selective; all falling → defensive.

### 🎯 Individual stock picks — the "extra boost" layer
**This is where active stock picks live in Zero's plan — explicitly, not as an add-on.** In the Build-a-Core-ETF step he lists the booster order: *core → satellites → and "**even we can add individual stock picking, for extra boost — if we are not satisfied with satellites**"* (transcript ~L105–108). So picks are the **optional top layer**, funded from the satellite/risk budget, sitting at the **tip of the pyramid (High-risk 5–10%)**.

The *way you run them* is the **active side of the Hybrid** (next section): they are **timed setups**, not buy-and-hold. Zero: *"you take a setup… like **CCL**, one setup, that's it"* (~L369) — `CCL` is one of his own stock signals, taken with an entry and an exit. **So "individual picks = active trades, entry + exit, 3–5 at a time" is faithful to Zero, not a deviation.** They earn their place only if the active sleeve actually beats the benchmark (Concept 4); otherwise the money belongs in passive core.

### Hybrid passive + active (Concept — Passive vs Active)
- **Passive** = minimal trading, buy & hold, compound; low stress, no time, benchmark-oriented. **Active** = frequent trading, TA, alpha-seeking; needs time + skill + emotional discipline.
- **Hybrid = both:** keep the **core always invested** (never fully out, ignore short-term noise) **+** a **selective active sleeve** (the individual picks above) that optimises entries on **HTF/MTF alignment** (IVB / ORB / Wyckoff / auction), **one setup at a time**, no overtrading. Split realised profits three ways: back into **core ETFs** / into **satellites** / into **enjoying the reward**. *"Act only when conditions align."*
- The regime dictates which side leads: *"market goes up → exposed via passive; market goes down → buy more via passive **and** hedge/swing via active."* You're never forced to choose timing **or** time-in-market — the hybrid does both.

### 📏 Performance (Concept 4)
- *"Without measurement it's not investing, it's guessing."* Gains feel like skill and losses like bad luck — only the benchmark tells the truth.
- You must **at minimum beat the passive benchmark.** 2020–2025 reference (cumulative / CAGR): **Nasdaq 100 +90% (~12–15%/yr)**, **S&P 500 +70% (~10–12%/yr)**, **MSCI ACWI +50% (~7–9%/yr)**. A portfolio up "only" +40% over that window is **underperforming** the index — more stress for less return is a **failure**, not a win.
- The honest question: *"Am I being paid, in extra return, for the extra time, skill and stress I'm taking on?"* If not, go passive.

## Target blueprint (quick reference)

**The model is two-level, not a flat list.** Level 1 = Solar System (Core vs Satellites, % of portfolio). Level 2 = the Pyramid, which lives **inside the satellite** and splits *that* budget by risk tier (% of satellite). Don't flatten them.

**Level 1 — Solar System (% of portfolio):**

| Sleeve | Band | Split | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core** | **60–80% (~70%)** | 40% all-world · 15% US · 15% quality-income | stable compounding engine; outside the pyramid |
| **Satellites** | **20–40%** | by theme ↓ | the boost; rotated by GDP cycle |
| — Tech/Innovation | 8–12% | | |
| — Emerging Markets | 5–8% | | |
| — Commodities (gold) | 5–8% | | physical ETC for Sharia |
| — Niche themes | 3–6% | | clean energy, biotech, gaming |
| — Crypto | 2–5% | | spot only for Sharia |
| **Cash** | 10–20% | | base of the pyramid; dry powder |

**Level 2 — Pyramid (% *of the satellite sleeve*, NOT of the portfolio):**

| Tier | % of satellite | What | In portfolio terms (sat=30%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High** | 5–10% | microcaps, turnarounds, **individual stock picks (active)** | ≈ **1.5–3% of portfolio** |
| **Moderate** | 25–35% | mid-cap discretionary growth | ≈ 7.5–10.5% |
| **Lower** | 30–40% | consumer staples | ≈ 9–12% |
| **Defensive** | 10–20% | utilities, healthcare, cash | ≈ 3–6% |

> **On individual picks:** they are the **High-risk tip of the satellite pyramid — 5–10% *of the satellite***, i.e. only ~**1.5–3% of the whole portfolio** at a 30% satellite weight. Zero gives them no standalone portfolio band; they're funded from the satellite risk budget and justified only if the active sleeve beats the benchmark. **The hub's flat `picks_pct: 0.12` (12% of the *portfolio*) is ~4–8× Zero's intended size** — this is a structural defect, not just a knob (see the hub-fidelity note below).

**Benchmark to beat:** S&P 500 ~10–12%/yr (or MSCI ACWI ~7–9%/yr global). Drawdown buy-ladder: −20% hold, −30% lean in, −50% max buy (if fundamentals intact).

## ⚠️ Where the hub diverges from this framework (structural, audited 2026-06-25)

An independent audit (codex, cross-checked against the transcript) found the hub's Allocation engine **flattens Zero's two-level model into one flat 5-sleeve list** (`core/sat_etf/crypto/picks/cash`, forced to sum to 100% — `targets.js`). Consequences:

1. **Pyramid collapsed.** Tier is only a per-ticker scoring input in `allocate.js`, never a *budget inside satellites*. You can't say "X% of satellites is high-risk", and rotation can't move capital up/down tiers.
2. **Crypto over-weighted.** Zero: crypto is one satellite at 2–5%. Hub: own top-level sleeve at 10%.
3. **Satellites hidden.** Tech/EM/commodities/niche are squashed into one `sat_etf` 10% sleeve; `fund-match.js` tags every satellite `moderate`, so gold loses its lower/defensive role.
4. **Picks mis-sized & incoherent.** `picks_pct` 12% of portfolio vs Zero's ~1.5–3%. And active trades **don't even consume the picks sleeve** — `ledger.js` excludes `layer:'trade'` from allocation and sizes them off `active_risk_cap_pct`, so the 12% sleeve actually funds *hold-layer* stock names while real picks run on a separate risk model.
5. **Rotation is decorative.** `rotation.js` computes a regime + `favorTiers`, but `allocateTargets` never takes it — it's a banner, not an allocator.

**The fix:** nest the model — Core + Satellites, satellites carrying both a theme band and a pyramid-tier budget; picks = the High tier of the satellite (not a peer sleeve); crypto capped at 2–5% inside satellites unless manually overridden with a warning; rotation feeds the tier weights. This is a design change, tracked separately — see the hub backlog.

## How this is adapted (Mansoor's actual approach)

The masterclass is a **flexible guide, not gospel** — it bends to fit. Mansoor's portfolio = **ETFs (the de-risking majority) + stable cash + Individual stock picks + crypto**. His **Individual Picks are ACTIVE TRADES** — Zero's stock signals where he finds an entry and an exit — *not* buy-and-hold. So in the hub the Picks sleeve shows active trades (with R:R), and the ETF sleeves (Core/Satellite) are the buy-and-hold de-risking base. Don't push "hold long-term" on the picks.

**Attribution, kept honest:** Zero runs picks as the active tip of the satellite — *"one setup at a time"* (his CCL example), High-risk **5–10% of the satellite** (~1.5–3% of portfolio). Mansoor's variation is to run **3–5 concurrently** and size the sleeve larger. That's a legitimate "bends to fit" choice — but it should be a *conscious* override of Zero's ~1.5–3%, not the silent default the hub currently ships (12% flat). Individual picking itself is squarely *in Zero's plan*; the **sizing and the flat-sleeve structure** are where the hub drifts from him.

## Practical learnings (this portfolio, applied)

- **Sharia + spot-only**: no leverage/perps; crypto spot only; gold via **physically-backed ETC**; sukuk replaces bonds. Every holding gets a Sharia rating (see [SHARIA-SCREENING](SHARIA-SCREENING.md)).
- **Trading 212 FX fee — 0.15%** on any currency conversion. Buying a **USD-quoted** line from a GBP ISA triggers it (e.g. ~£13 on an £8.6k buy). **Avoid it by buying the GBP/GBX-quoted LSE line** of the same fund. T212's `.GB` suffix means *LSE listing*, **not** GBP currency — check the currency on the buy screen.
- **Halal GBP-quoted (no-FX) lines that exist:**
  - **Core world:** `ISWD` (iShares MSCI World Islamic, GBP) ✅ — use instead of `ISDW` (USD twin). `HIWS` (HSBC World Islamic Screened, GBP) ✅.
  - **Emerging markets:** `HIES` (HSBC EM Islamic, GBP) ✅, `ISDE` (iShares EM Islamic — GBP LSE line; verify GBP at buy).
  - **Gold:** `SGLN` (iShares Physical Gold, GBP, unhedged) ✅, `IGLG` (iShares Physical Gold **GBP-hedged**) ✅.
  - **No GBP line exists:** Sharia **tech** (SPTE etc. are US/USD only) — but the world Core already holds big tech, so a dedicated tech satellite is optional. USA-Islamic (`ISUS`) and US-listed SP Funds (`SPUS`, `SPSK`, `HLAL`) are USD-only too.
- **Benchmark for the book:** MSCI World Islamic (`ISDW.L` on the price feed) — the "beat the index" scorecard.
