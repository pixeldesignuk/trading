## 0. PRIME DIRECTIVES (the laws everything else serves)

1. **Market structure rules above everything** — fundamentals, indicators, news all bow to it.
2. **Protect capital first, grow second.** Profit is the reward for survival. Your job isn't to be right; it's to manage being wrong.
3. **HTF is king.** The higher the timeframe, the more weight its structure carries. Institutions trade HTF; retail loses on LTF noise.
4. **Closures > wicks.** Wicks are liquidity hunts. The body is the truth.
5. **Never chase. Never long a breakout.** Wait for the pullback into a valid level — or forget the trade.
6. **Buy discount, sell premium.** Compound below equilibrium, trim above it.
7. **Think in probabilities, not predictions.** One trade is one sample in a large series.
8. **Fundamentals = 10%, price action = 90%.** Fundamentals are a "shinigami" — comprehensible justifications engineered to move the herd.
9. **Counter-trade the majority.** News bottoms = max FUD; tops = max FOMO. Ask "who profits?"
10. **The market adapts to your life, not your life to the market.** Trading is complementary income, never the lifeline.

## 2. MARKET STRUCTURE (the master concept)

**4 macro phases:** Accumulation (sideways after downtrend, SM buys) → **Mark-up** (retail FOMO) → Distribution (sideways after uptrend, SM sells) → **Mark-down** (traps late buyers).

**3 states:** Bullish = HH+HL · Bearish = LH+LL · Ranging = neither broken.

**Swings (the blueprint):** A valid swing must stand out *structurally* (clear rejection + momentum shift, flanked by lower-highs/higher-lows). HTF swings > LTF spikes.
- **SH holds BSL** (retail short-stops above) — SM pushes through to grab, then reverses.
- **SL holds SSL** (retail long-stops below) — SM dips to collect, then reverses.

**BOS vs CHoCH:** BOS = continuation (price closes beyond prior swing in trend direction). CHoCH = first reversal sign (break *against* trend after exhaustion + failure to continue). *All CHoCH are BOS; not all BOS are CHoCH.*

**THE CLOSURE RULE for any break:** must close with the **body beyond** the level, not just a wick. Wick through + close back inside = **fakeout / liquidity grab**. When watching a daily SH, only a **daily/weekly close** confirms — never trust a 1H/4H close for an HTF level.

**New swing > old swing:** when price fails to close above a SH, the *new* failure point becomes the live battleground (recency + fresh liquidity).

**Breakout→retest is mandatory:** BOS + HTF close → wait for pullback into a level (OB/demand/fib) → clean BOS off it. Chasing the breakout = donating liquidity to SM.

## 3. CANDLES & CLOSURES (forget pattern names)

It's not what the candle is *called*, it's what it *shows*. Body = conviction; wicks = manipulation/indecision.

**Closure strength (memorize the fractions):**
| Closure | Body vs range | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strong** | ≥ **2/3** | One side dominated | Trade with it |
| **Moderate** | ≥ **1/2** | Momentum + rejection | Wait 1–2 candles |
| **Weak** | < **1/3** | Indecision | **No trade** |

Only named exceptions he respects: **Marubozu** (no wicks = pure displacement, leaves liquidity) and **Engulfing** (only reliable after a mature trend; an engulf *with* the trend = continuation, not reversal).

**Confusion resolver:** confused on a TF → go up one (1H→4H→1D→1W) until a clean closure above/below a key level appears. No clarity = wait.

## 4. TIMEFRAMES

HTF = D/W/M (less noise, institutional). MTF = 4H. LTF = <4H (market-maker games). **Top-down always.** HTF defines bias & levels; LTF only refines entry. Closures at **1D / 1W / 1M act as watersheds.** Confirmation must come on the **same-or-higher TF** as the level being tested.

## 5. FIBONACCI & PREMIUM/DISCOUNT

**Draw:** uptrend = swing low→high; downtrend = swing high→low.
**Levels he uses:** 0.5 (EQ), **0.618 (golden pocket)**, **0.705 ("algorithm fib," strongest pair w/ 0.5)**, 0.786, 0.886. Rarely 0.236/0.382. **Targets/extensions:** 1.272, 1.454, 1.618 (and -0.618/-1 for unexplored upside).
**Map:** below 0.5 = **discount (buy)** · above 0.5 = **premium (sell)** · 0.5 = equilibrium watershed.
**Golden zone entry:** 0.382–0.618 healthy pullback. **OTE/OTZ (ICT):** entry 0.62–0.79 **only with an FVG** on the left for confluence; TPs at -0.27/-0.62/-1.
**Confluence stack:** 0.5 + 0.618 + 0.705 + 0.786 overlapping a liquidity zone = high-confidence. HTF fibs > LTF fibs.

## 6. SUPPLY/DEMAND · ORDER BLOCKS · BREAKERS · HOBs

**S/D zones** = areas (not lines) at the origin of an impulse out of consolidation. **More tests = weaker** (orders consumed). Read **dominance** (who makes the HH/HL structure) + **momentum** (speed to the zone — faster = stronger).

**Valid Order Block — 4 conditions (all required):**
1. **Liquidity grab** before it (wick taking stops).
2. **Strong body candle** (big body, minimal wick; multi-candle OK if same impulse → mark OBH/OBL).
3. **BOS** of the prior swing after it. *(No BOS → no OB.)*
4. **Imbalance/FVG** left behind (3rd candle doesn't fill the gap).

**Mitigation:** **Unmitigated = strongest** (fresh liquidity). Small wick touch = still live; violent body-through = mitigated/dead.

**Breaker Block:** a failed OB that flips polarity (old supply → demand on reclaim, and vice-versa). Double/triple breakers = repeatedly defended = stronger. When an OB melts into a breaker it becomes a "defensive bulwark" → trail SL behind it.

**Hidden OB (HOB):** institutional orders disguised as consolidation, living **exclusively behind FVGs**. Use **body open/close only** (ignore wicks) of 2 consecutive candles (first opens within second's body); the **mid-zone = exact entry** (align with fib). **Stacked FVGs above a HOB** = compressed energy → sharp reaction on the tap.

## 7. LIQUIDITY & REFINING

Liquidity = clustered orders (stops, TPs). SM moves price to where retail stops sit, grabs them, reverses. **BSL above highs, SSL below lows.** Inducement = an obvious near level that lures retail before the real grab.

**Refining workflow (HTF zone → LTF precision):**
1. Mark HTF imbalance/zone (W / 2D).
2. Drop to a cleaner mid-TF (2D→23H) for tidy candles.
3. Mark **fresh** HOBs behind FVGs.
4. Add fib confluence (HTF fib + LTF fib).
5. Limit order at mid-zone, or wait for next MTF/HTF body close then market/DCA. First touch = limit OK; second touch = market justified (level proven).

He hunts liquidity on **uncommon TFs** retail can't see: 2D/3D/4D/5D/6D/2W/3W (HTF) and desync LTFs 23H, 22H, 20–21H, 12H, 10H, 8H, plus the **"Dragon" 1390-min (23h10m)** to bypass broker daily-close OBs.

## 8. VOLUME · AMT · VOLUME PROFILE

**Auction Market Theory:** markets move on **imbalance** until they find **fair value** (tight range, high volume, balanced — auction pauses). Imbalances are **magnets** (low-volume voids price returns to fill; can take weeks/months). *If price rebalances an imbalance it has fuel to continue; if it can't, it lacks fuel.* Value has memory; old accepted areas pull price back.

**Volume Profile (use Fixed-Range from swing→swing):**
- **POC** = most-traded price (fair value magnet). **VAH/VAL** = 70% value-area bounds (supply/demand on retest). **HVN** = magnet/strong S-R. **LVN** = "highway," price accelerates.
- Retest plays: hold above **VAL** = bullish continuation; reject at **VAH** = bearish.
- **Absorption rule:** executed sell>buy at a level but price rises = resting buy orders absorbing → continuation. Raw executed volume ≠ direction.
- **Profile shapes:** D = balance · P = short-covering (bull continuation) · b = long-liquidation (bear continuation) · double = transition.

**VSA quick read:** big body+high vol = real · small body+low vol = fakeout · **big body+low vol = trap** · **small body+high vol = absorption (SM stepping in).**

**FVG:** the chart gap manifesting an imbalance; price returns to fill it (path of least resistance).

## 9. WYCKOFF (the "why" behind the zones)

**Laws:** Supply/Demand · Cause & Effect (bigger base = bigger move) · **Effort vs Result** (high volume + small move = weakness) · Composite Man (SM orchestrates phases to trap retail).

**Accumulation:** PS → **SC** (panic low, vol spike) → AR → ST → [Phase B range, longest] → **Spring** (false breakdown grabs stops on low vol, fast recovery) → SOS/**LPS** (entry) → breakout (mark-up).
**Distribution (inverse):** BC → AR → ST → range → **UTAD** (false breakout up) → LPSY/SOW → mark-down.
**Volume confirm:** accumulation → vol expands on up moves; distribution → vol expands on down moves; **retest on low volume = control has shifted.** Bad news + price won't drop = accumulation; good news + price won't rise = distribution.

**ICT overlays:** PDA = Consolidation→Expansion→Retracement→Reversal. **PO3** = Accumulation→Manipulation (sweep)→Distribution (real move); bullish PO3 deviates *below* a key swing first, bearish *above*. **MMXM** = triple-tap liquidity then reversal. **Unicorn** = liquidity grab + OB→breaker + overlapping FVG.

## 11. THE STRATEGY (end-to-end playbook)

**Philosophy:** structure-first, emotion-second. No "Pokémon trading" (don't chase every coin). 3–5 core spot names, deliberately few. Never >50% invested — always dry powder.

**4-step engine:**
1. **Macro context** — bear/bull, HTF discount/premium (vs 0.5 EQ of the HTF range).
2. **Mark every HTF level** — 12M→1D: supplies, demands, OBs, breakers, EQ (use uncommon TFs). **Assign each level a forced exit %** (e.g., trim 5%/5%/10%/20% into EQ). No emotion at the level — mechanical trim.
3. **Identify the Wyckoff phase** around those zones (accumulation/distribution) to know *why* a zone should hold and when the breakout/markdown comes (Phase E).
4. **Refine entry on LTF** — HOBs behind FVGs + fib confluence + liquidity event → run the entry checklist → manage with trailing SL.

**HTF spot accumulation (building ground):** start in bear/discount, asset ≥ -60% from ATH. Define range high→low, EQ = 50%, buy only below EQ. **DCA total budget into 10–20 equal parts** spread across discount levels (don't try to catch the bottom). Log every fill + running average.

**Scale-in (low leverage "zen mode"):** within a wide HTF zone, sub-levels 1/2/3 → 0.5% / 1% / 1.5%. Initial SL below the whole zone → move to BE on first target → trail behind new breakers → re-accumulate on pullbacks while thesis holds.

**Hedging (discipline, not doubt):** hedge max 1–2 positions; fund the hedge from partial-TP profits (no new capital); hedge size = ¼–½ of trimmed profit; short profit buys more spot at discount.

## 12. RISK & POSITION SIZING

> ⚠️ **Scope:** §11–13 are the **leveraged / crypto / bull-season** trading framework (tight invalidation stops, margin sizing, 3–5 single-shot positions). For **traditional-market spot investing** (ISA/JISA stocks, no leverage), this does **not** apply — use the **[Investing Masterclass](INVESTING-MASTERCLASS.md)** instead: solar-system core/satellite allocation, pyramid-of-risk tiers, macro rotation, and beating the benchmark. The two are complementary, not interchangeable.

- **Risk per trade by TF:** HTF up to **3%**, MTF **2%**, LTF **≤1%** (smaller if setup suboptimal). Risk measured **entry→stop**, not margin/notional.
- **Position size = Risk Amount ÷ Stop-Loss Distance.** (e.g., $1,000 acct × 2% = $20 risk; 5% SL → $20/0.05 = **$400 position**.)
- **R:R minimum 2:1.** Edge comes from R:R, not win rate (40% wins at 3:1 is profitable).
- **Leverage account ≈ 1/5 of spot**, tactical only. Leverage is a precision tool, not a lottery ticket — small size = no revenge trading.
- Inexperienced → trade **HTF/MTF only**. Capital limited → don't raise risk %; compound / earn more.

## 13. STOP LOSS

**Rule #1: capital preservation.** A break of the relevant SH/SL = invalidation, period.
**Placement methods:** (a) beyond previous swing; (b) breakout candle ± **1.5× ATR(14)**; (c) beyond the FVG/OB low (e.g. below prior support under the OB); (d) beyond the whole liquidity zone + **2% buffer**; (e) liquidity + fib extension (127.2/145.4/161.8) projection.
**Never:** fixed-% stops (5/10%), round numbers, obvious retail clusters, mental-only stops, or *widening* a stop once hit. **Trail to BE** once in profit, then follow each BOS.

## 14. ENTRIES, SESSIONS & EXECUTION

**Orders:** **Limit (his default)** for HTF precision (no slippage; lets you live your life) vs **Market** for MTF/LTF. Split capital across levels (e.g., 30/40/30 at L1/EQ/L3).

**Sessions (UTC):** Asian 00–09 (builds the range) · London 07–16 (sweeps the range, sets direction) · **London–NY overlap 13–22 = biggest moves** · NY 16–22 (most lucrative).
**Asian-range → London-sweep → NY-reversal** is the core intraday template.

**NY Open playbook:** BTC pumps pre-NYO → opens dump (and vice-versa). **1st move (first ~15–30 min) is usually fake (liquidity grab); 2nd move is real.** Trade NYO only if a valid D/S zone exists — never just because it's NYO.

**Management:** pre-set TPs **before** entry (robotic, automated/alerts). Partial at marked zones, trail the rest. Hit TP → sell, no debate. Invalidated → exit, reassess, re-enter.

## 19. PSYCHOLOGY (the real edge)

**Why > How:** trade from passion for the craft, not Lamborghinis — passion → process → profit, never reversed.
**5 pillars:** Practice · Acceptance · Discipline · Responsibility · Self-understanding.
**Probabilistic mind:** stop trying to be *right*; manage being wrong. One trade says nothing about you — only whether you followed process. Survival first; the market can't be controlled, only yourself.

**Biases → fixes:** FOMO → research from PA, then disconnect 2 weeks after selling · Overtrading → patience is the edge; inaction is often correct · Revenge → **Kyūkei**: set a 2–3% loss threshold, step away · Greed → pre-set SL/TP, trail · Overconfidence → wins test discipline too · Fear/paralysis → losses are data (**Kintsugi**: strength from the wound).

**Routine:** Before (review/plan) · During (execute, journal every decision) · After (review wins/losses) · + Disconnect · + Gratitude. **Shugyō** = emotion journal; **Hansei** = own the mistake's cause.

**Emotional management:** positive reframing · **disconnect** from socials/price (it's a FOMO magnet) · workout 3×/wk · gratitude · clean diet · **never obsessively check the market** — 90% of losses come not from the entry but from the weakness to rush back in.

**Compounding math (the point):** 2% risk, ~0.5–1%/trade, ~50 trades/yr → $1k becomes ~$1,643 (Y1), ~$2,653 (Y5), ~$7,400 (Y10). Chasing $1k→$50k in a month is account-ruin by design. Small roots grow the tallest trees.

## 20. SIGNAL EVALUATION FRAMEWORK
_How to grade any Telegram/influencer signal against Zero's method. Score each; a Zero-aligned setup should pass most. Educational scoring only._

**A. First, vet the source (§ scamfluencer red flags):**
- ❌ Coin lists with no reasoning · undisclosed paid shills · the same tickers pushed simultaneously across "unrelated" accounts (coordination) · perfectly-timed calls at obvious tops · micro-cap "hidden gems." **Ask: who profits from this signal?**

**B. Score the setup (10 checks — Zero-aligned = green):**
| # | Check | ✅ Pass | ❌ Fail / Red flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Direction vs EQ** | Long in HTF **discount** / short in **premium** | Long in premium, short in discount |
| 2 | **Valid zone** | Entry at OB/HOB/demand/FVG/fib confluence | Entry at random price / "market now" |
| 3 | **Liquidity event** | Sweep/grab of BSL/SSL before entry | Entering into untouched liquidity |
| 4 | **Structure** | BOS/CHoCH in trade direction | Counter-structure, no shift |
| 5 | **HTF closure** | Confirmed on same-or-higher TF as the level | "1H closed above" a daily level |
| 6 | **Not a chase** | Pullback/retest entry | Chasing a breakout / parabolic candle |
| 7 | **Stop logic** | Structure-based (beyond swing/zone +buffer) | Fixed % / round number / no SL |
| 8 | **R:R** | ≥ **2:1**, ideally 3:1 | <2:1 or undefined target |
| 9 | **Macro confluence** | Aligns w/ BTC trend, BTC.D, USDT.D, funding | Ignores the tape; funding/OI against it |
| 10 | **Sizing/management** | Defined risk %, pre-set TPs, trail plan | "All in," no plan, moving stops |

**C. Verdict scale:**
- **8–10 ✅** = textbook Zero setup (study why it works).
- **5–7 ⚠️** = partial; note exactly which pillars are missing.
- **≤4 ❌** = not his method; likely a chase, a premium-long, or shill. The instructive value is *naming the violations*.

**D. Always reframe the lesson:** for every signal, state (1) where it sits vs EQ, (2) what liquidity it's targeting, (3) what would invalidate it, (4) whether news/hype is being used as a trap, (5) the Zero rule it obeys or breaks. That's the educational rep — not the P&L.
